a poster exploring the pandemic experience; even when we attempt to recreate our pre-covid lives within isolation, they are only poor imitations of what once was.

with the pandemic requiring home isolation, my daily-use items have changed in response. these objects are translated into 2D forms that can be assembled into 3D paper duplicates. in doing so, the audience can attempt to quarantine as i do. with their artefact-riddled textures, simplified geometry, and the inevitable mistakes of assembly, the resulting paper models will vaguely resemble their original form.

an interactive collection of forum posts exploring taste. curate the visible conversations to either create your own poem, or journey through the web of forum posts to get to know the users.

the curated dining-out experience has disappeared during the pandemic. hot becomes cold, and crispy becomes soggy, thanks to delivery and takeout. a forum topic about dental appliances causing taste changes evokes a similar sentiment to this. though these two situations are uncontrollable and inevitable as individuals, allowing the user to create their own poem of the experience is a way to give power back to the individual, in a therapeutic and fun manner.







the metamorphosis by f kafka, becomes an eccentric print workbook/resource. it is through the main character's journey of mental suffering and physical anguish that the reader is given tools and resources to help them in their own struggles.

there are 17 editions of kafka's well known piece. thus, this layout is intended to be a visual journey that embodies the novella's absurdity. taking cues from theatre of the absurd, nonsensical yet relevant things such as links to job searches or official mental health diagnostic quizzes encircle the text. in the background sits the original german text, in a green that signals mould and poison.

an imperfect pangram of all the letters of the english alphabet, whose glyphs are created solely with code.

making use of algorithms to create images can produce consistent and surprisingly beautiful results. this exercise aims to explore the beauty that a computer can create without using photoshop and other big-name software. using the package p5.js and a lot of elbow grease, these analog-esque images came fourth from the input of liberation sans letters.

a digital interface breaking down the meaning of aesthetics relative to our interactions. by changing how we spatially and temporally experience flowers, how does our immediate relationship change?

for little to no tangible benefit, we provide flowers care and comfort purely due to their beauty. this is in contrast to our relationships with other humans, where bonds are forged through social, emotional, and intellectual points. thus, take away a flower's physicality and what happens to our relationship with them? together with the 5 stages of relationships, the deconstruction and reconstruction aims to explore the physicality and mentality of what makes a bond.
